C2C Industry Briefing

Past 7 days · to 4 Jul 2026
The theme this week is fee models converging on "free to sell" and pressure building in Australia — one of your markets. Depop is dropping its 10% seller commission there (22 July), pushing costs onto a new buyer marketplace fee, hard on the heels of eBay's own fee-free AU launch and Vinted's quiet AU entry. Meanwhile the eBay–Depop deal still hangs on the UK CMA (decision due 6 Aug), and eBay Germany is raising some new-goods fees to subsidise its recommerce push.
What it means for you: across DE, UK and AU the direction is identical — zero seller fees, revenue shifted to buyer-side fees, managed shipping, and PayPal being phased out where in-house payments are mandatory. "Free to sell" is becoming table stakes in your markets; the competitive fight is moving to buyer experience and take-rate mechanics, not seller acquisition.
